# Research Methodology: Target Company Identification (Indore Agri-Tech)
**Project:** DeepThought Business Analytics Internship – Part A  
**Candidate:** Yash Raj Gupta  

---

## 1. How I Found the Companies
The identification process was built on a **top-down industrial mapping** strategy. Instead of searching for "seed companies" generically, I targeted the specific industrial ecosystems where "Federer" companies thrive.

* **Geographic Focus:** I mapped the **Indore-Pithampur Industrial Corridor** (specifically Pithampur SEZ and the Sanwer Road Industrial Estate). This cluster is strategically significant due to its proximity to the **Indian Institute of Soybean Research (IISR)** in Indore, which creates a natural spillover of technical talent and research.
* **Technological Markers:** I searched for "Biotech Hubs" and "Tissue Culture Labs" in Central India. Companies in these hubs are inherently more likely to have the in-house R&D capability required for the Federer profile compared to bulk commodity traders.

## 2. Sources Used
To ensure high data integrity, I cross-referenced information across four distinct source types:
* **Government Registries:** * **DSIR (Dept. of Scientific & Industrial Research):** Used to identify companies with recognized in-house R&D units. 
    * **MCA21 (Ministry of Corporate Affairs):** Verified "Manufacturer" status (C1) and "India-based" (C2) through Director details and Registered Office addresses.
* **Financial Portals:** **Tofler** and **ZaubaCorp** were used to verify the **Rs. 50Cr–Rs. 500Cr revenue band**. This was critical for identifying the "Strategic Fails" (companies that grew too large, like Sonic Biochem).
* **Technical/Industry Data:** * **Seed Association of India / MP Seed Association:** Member directories helped verify "C3: Differentiation."
    * **National Horticulture Board (NHB):** Used to find recognized tissue culture laboratories in the MP region.
* **Professional Networking:** **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** was used to verify **C4 (Technical DM)** by checking if founders had degrees in Agronomy, Biotechnology, or Chemical Engineering.

## 3. The Research Process
My process followed a strict four-step pipeline to filter the initial "Universe" down to the final 25:

1.  **Sourcing (The Universe):** Generated a list of ~85 companies in the Indore region using the sources above.
2.  **Hard Filters (Pre-Scoring):** Immediately removed any company with "Trading," "Export-Import," or "Distributor" in the name.
3.  **The 6-Criteria Deep Dive:** Evaluated the remaining companies against the Federer Rubric (C1-C6). I looked for specific evidence like "Proprietary genetics," "USFDA/EU-GMP approval," or "Gen-2 leaders with technical Master’s degrees."
4.  **Triage & Yield Management:** * **Band A (Strong Pass):** Score 80-100.
    * **Band D (Strategic Fail):** Included to demonstrate the ability to identify "PE-ownership" or "Revenue Cap" disqualifiers.

## 4. Segment Learnings: Hybrid Seeds & Agri-Inputs
Throughout the research, I observed several key insights about the Indore agri-manufacturing segment:
* **The R&D Pivot:** Many traditional seed processors in Indore are currently in a "Capability Upgrade" phase. They are moving from basic seed grading to high-barrier biotech (like microbial fermentation and tissue culture).
* **Soybean Dominance:** Because Indore is India's "Soybean Bowl," the most successful Federated companies are those building proprietary genetics for oilseeds to reduce India's import dependency.
* **The Scale Ceiling:** I found a high concentration of companies at the Rs. 300Cr–400Cr mark. This segment often lacks structured execution systems to jump to the >Rs. 500Cr tier, which validates why DeepThought’s "Scientific Execution" is a strong fit for this specific market.
